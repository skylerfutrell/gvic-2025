Annual Gun Violence Statistics

  • Over 40,000 people die from gun violence in the United States each year.
  • Approximately 115 people die from gun violence each day in America.
  • For every person killed with guns, two more are wounded.
  • Firearms are the leading cause of death for children and teens in the United States.
  • More than 500 people die in mass shootings in an average year.

Demographics & Impact

  • Black Americans are 10 times more likely than white Americans to die by gun homicide.
  • Men account for 86% of gun deaths and 87% of non-fatal gun injuries.
  • Gun suicide rates are highest among older white men.
  • In an average month, 70 women are shot and killed by an intimate partner.
  • Gun violence costs the U.S. $557 billion annually, representing 2.6% of GDP.

Key Facts About Gun Violence

  • The U.S. has the highest rate of gun deaths among high-income countries.
  • Access to a gun increases the risk of death by suicide by three times.
  • States with stronger gun laws have lower gun death rates.
  • Nearly two-thirds of gun deaths are suicides.
  • An estimated 4.6 million American children live in homes with at least one unlocked and loaded firearm.

Sources: CDC, Gun Violence Archive, Everytown Research, American Journal of Public Health
